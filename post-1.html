How I Built My First Portfolio

Posted on October 20, 2025 • 5 min read

Building my first portfolio website was both exciting and challenging. I wanted something that reflected my personality, was easy to navigate, and showcased my work effectively.

1. Starting with a Plan

I began by sketching wireframes on paper. This helped me define the basic layout: a hero section, project cards, and a contact form.

2. Writing Clean, Reusable Code

Instead of styling each section separately, I used global CSS classes for typography, spacing, and layout consistency.

3. Adding Personality with Animations

Small hover effects and subtle animations made the site feel more alive without overwhelming visitors.

“Your portfolio isn't just a website — it's your digital handshake.”

In the end, I learned how to combine creativity and structure. The result was a site that truly represented who I am as a developer.
